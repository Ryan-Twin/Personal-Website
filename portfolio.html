Design Focus

Personal Website

Developed from scratch, this personal website is a product of my prior experience with design and web programming. It utilizes primarily HTML5, CSS and Javascript, running on top of Bootstrap, and AWS S3/Route 53 for hosting. Naturally, its design reflects on my own aesthetic interests, which were realized using Photoshop.

You can find the source code on Github.

YouQueue Wireframes

An assignment for my Intro to GUI Programming/Graphics course, this set of wireframes is a perfect example of my ability to design simple and efficient user experiences. They illustrate a tool designed to enhance watching videos at a gathering, by giving a group of friends a queue for enabling video submissions through mobile devices. After its initial iteration, it was then updated using partner-gathered feedback and redesigned.

The updated wireframes are available to view at Balsamiq.

Implementation Focus

Remittance Reporter

Over the course of Summer 2020, I interned at CollBox, a small startup in the live music capital of the world, Austin, TX. While I developed many of my skills there, I spent the majority of my time working on a Remittance Reporter, or simply put, a tool to help keep track of payments received by their partners. The tool is written in Clojure, from the ground up.

References and sample code are available upon request.
